Egyptian Cuisine

Ful Medames is Egypt’s ancient breakfast staple, simmered fava beans spiced with cumin, garlic, and lemon, fueled pharaohs and still warms the soul of every modern Egyptian.
Koshari is a hearty clash of lentils, rice, pasta, and crispy onions, crowned with spicy tomato sauce—Egypt’s beloved street food, born of ingenuity and cultural fusion.
Molokhia is a Velvety green soup steeped in history, once forbidden for commoners, now a national comfort dish—jute leaves cooked with garlic, coriander, and served over rice or bread.
